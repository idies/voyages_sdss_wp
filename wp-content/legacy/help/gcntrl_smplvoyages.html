 The website, Voyages, was designed to provide teachers flexibility in the areas of time and approach.  The result is a shopping cart experience  that allows you to choose  from three distinctly different activity types to create a unique learning sequence - a voyage - that suite your instructional needs.

Preflight Training

Preflight trainings are skill-based. They are designed to take no more than a single class period and highlight very focused content. Most Preflight Trainings cover the essential elements of a topic.  They can be used as references when you get stuck or set the stage for success in more complex work in the Launch and Expeditions sections.

Activities in this section are designed to take no more than three class periods and involve the discovery of some astronomy or engineering concept using SDSS data. They do not require the use of outside spreadsheet programs and so they can work well with lower level students.

Expeditions

These longer-term projects may require one or more weeks to complete and target students who already have a background in astronomy.  These projects are ideal for motivated high school students or university students taking an introductory course in astronomy.

 

Because of their different designs, you can find the same content approached with different learning objectives in mind.  For example, in the Launch - Star Color, students discover, through observation, the nature of the magnitude scale while other activities might require this knowledge as a prerequisite. In latter, Preflight Magnitude might be a useful homework assignment before getting started or as a reference. In  Expedition - Galaxy Color the concepts of magnitude and color are required to construct computer queries to answer questions that expand upon the Hubble tuning fork diagram.

 

Which activities you choose to make part of your voyage depends upon your unique situation. To decide what elements you need to create the ideal learning sequence for your students, start with the question: What do you need from Voyages?

I need Voyages to provide:

• supplemental content-specific activities and resources to fit into my established curriculum

•  a pathway to astronomy content over an extended time frame

•  enrichment experiences for small groups of students or individuals

•  content pathway to explore information technology and engineering

•  research experiences for individuals or groups

 

Once you are clear about what you need - and it may be a combination of things depending upon the group you are working with - you can choose the experiences that will work best for you. There are many ways to combine the current selection of activities and resources.  In this next section we provide sample voyages so you can get some ideas about how you might organize the options. Remember, Voyages is a growing resource.  Don't hesitate to send us your wish list and/or comments to sdss.voyages@gmail.com.
