We are happy to present a set of activities, resources and research experiences, which build on data from the Sloan Digital Sky Survey. Our materials allow your students to explore a wide variety of astronomical objects, from asteroids in our own Solar System to the most distant quasars ever observed, using the same high-quality data employed by professional astronomers.

 

What is available in Voyages?

 

SkyServer Voyages is designed for students in high school and introductory college courses.  The entire site is designed to give teachers flexibility when organizing learning experiences for their students.  More than that, the structure of the lessons and resources provide many opportunities to differentiate on the fly.  Support material is available when needed as well as extensions. Students who have greater background and experience with the material can discover on there own the next area of exploration.  Students are given clues and directions throughout the activities about where to find more information or support to understand difficult concepts. Voyages strives to put students at the helm of their adventure with support from you at ground control.

 

This is your exploration.  Voyage provides you with places to go and the support material needed to make the experience diverse, rich and flexible.  The website is divided into the following sections:

Preflight Training - These activities are short learning experiences that have a known outcome, a right answer.  They address common astronomy concepts using the Sloan Digital Sky Survey database as the primary learning platform. Think about the skills you need before getting started.  These are the must-knows that you can't do without. Preflight Training activities are designed so they can be completed independently.  They are ideal as homework assignments before launching into a more complex activity.

    Preflight Trainings are skill-based.  Short quizzes to check student learning are embedded into each Preflight, providing students immediate feedback about their understanding of each topic.  Students can print a certificate of completion at the end of the lesson, providing a tangible ticket to greater adventures.  Preflight requirements for each Launch and Expedition level activity are identified directly under the activity or project title.  Teacher guides for each activity provide more ideas about how and when Preflight trainings can be used.

    A variety of Preflight Training experiences are available in a number of different areas, including:

•  Locations in the sky

•  The nature and behavior of light

•  The SDSS telescope, instruments and engineering

•  The SDSS database

Launch - These activities are designed to take no more than three class periods and involve the discovery of an astronomy or engineering concept using SDSS data.  For example, in the Launch activity, Star Color, students use their Preflight training in filters to discover the  magnitude and the need for multiple filter measurements to compare star colors.

    The Launch section itself is structured to support the development of an accurate mental model of the Universe. Launch activities are arranged by distance from Earth.

Launch to SDSS - This section enables students to explore the SDSS telescope and its associated instruments.

Launch to the Solar System  Activities in this section explore objects found within our own solar system, such as asteroids and planets.

Launch to the Milky Way - Almost all of the stars in the SDSS database reside within our own galaxy, the Milky Way.  This is an important understanding for students to acquire.  When we observe a galaxy, you can rarely pick out individual stars.  Grouping all activities that have stars as their subject in this section helps to reinforce this understanding.

Launch to the Cosmos - Millions of galaxies and hundreds of thousands of quasars far beyond our own Milky Way have been observed by the SDSS telescope.  Activities in this section provide explorations into the concept of redshift, and the properties and distributions of galaxies and distant quasars.

Expeditions If you have time to devote to following historical discoveries in astronomy or developing an in depth understanding of  astrophysics concepts, check out Expeditions. In general these projects require two or more weeks to complete and are targeted toward students who already have a background in basic astronomy.  These projects are ideal for motivated high school students or university students taking an introductory course in astronomy.  Most Expeditions require the use of outside programs like Excel.  Prior experience working with spreadsheets is very helpful.  Expeditions cover topics such as:

• The Hertzsprung-Russell Diagram

• The structure of the Universe

• Redshift

• Quasars as probes of intergalactic space

 

Help Voyages contains an extensive help section to support student acquisition of material and resources to explore further any tool or topic covered in Preflight Training, Launch or Expeditions.  The Help section includes:

• User guides for all the major SkyServer tools

• Video tutorials with tips and tricks for using SkyServer tools

• A complete SDSS glossary of terms designed for students new to astronomy

• Resources for the curious like "How to write a query" and "How did they build that?"

• Help is a growing all the time.  We welcome your suggestions for new topics!

 

What materials are available for teachers?

 

Teacher's pages are available for all lessons. The teacher's pages include:

 

•  Notes and teaching tips on all lessons

•  Sample solutions and scoring rubrics for all questions and exercises

•  Strategies to help you fit the lessons into your available time and learning objectives

•  Correlations to national Math and Science Standards

•  Strategies for documenting student progress from reproducible and editable student journals to on-line options.

•  Access to lesson plans and resources related to topics covered in Voyages through the Zoo Teach website.

 

How much does it cost?

 

Nothing!

 

All materials on SkyServer are available free of charge, including all lesson plans, access to the teacher's pages, and access to all data.

 

So, how do I get started?

 

If you are new to this website begin in the Unit Plans section which provides an overview of the lessons that are available.  It also contains a decision tree that will help guide you to the lessons and resources that are best suited to the group you are working with, the time you have available and the goals of your unit.
